# Lesson 1: Introductions and Asking for Names

One of the first things you'll learn in Cantonese is how to introduce yourself and ask for someone's name.

A very common phrase is:

**你叫乜嘢名？** `nei5 giu3 mat1 je5 meng2?`

> What is your name?

### Let's break it down

- **你 (nei5)** = you
- **叫 (giu3)** = to be called
- **乜嘢 (mat1 je5)** = what
- **名 (meng2)** = name

Literally:

> "You are called what name?"

---

## Saying Your Name

To tell someone your name, use:

**我叫 (ngo5 giu3) + name**

Examples:

- **我叫阿明。** `ngo5 giu3 aa3 ming4`
- **我叫阿美。** `ngo5 giu3 aa3 mei5`
- **我叫陳大文。** `ngo5 giu3 can4 daai6 man4`

This is one of the most important beginner sentence patterns in Cantonese.

---

## Using 呢 (ne1)

The particle **呢** is commonly used to mean:

> "What about you?"

Example:

**A:** 我叫阿明。 `ngo5 giu3 aa3 ming4`

(My name is Ming.)

**B:** 我叫阿美。你呢？ `ngo5 giu3 aa3 mei5. nei5 ne1?`

(My name is Mei. What about you?)

**A:** 我叫阿明。 `ngo5 giu3 aa3 ming4`

(My name is Ming.)

Notice that:

**你呢？** `nei5 ne1?`

is extremely common in everyday Cantonese and should be memorized early.

---

## Being Polite with 請問 (cing2 man6)

To make a question more polite, Cantonese speakers often begin with:

**請問** `cing2 man6`

### Breakdown

- **請** = please
- **問** = ask

Together, **請問** means:

> "May I ask..."
>
> "Excuse me..."

Example:

**請問，你叫乜嘢名？** `cing2 man6, nei5 giu3 mat1 je5 meng2?`

(May I ask what your name is?)

---

## Dialogue 1

**A:** 你好。 `nei5 hou2`

**B:** 你好。 `nei5 hou2`

**A:** 你叫乜嘢名？ `nei5 giu3 mat1 je5 meng2?`

**B:** 我叫阿美。你呢？ `ngo5 giu3 aa3 mei5. nei5 ne1?`

**A:** 我叫阿明。 `ngo5 giu3 aa3 ming4`

---

## Dialogue 2

**A:** 你好。請問，你叫乜嘢名？

**B:** 我叫陳小玲。

**A:** 好高興認識你。

**B:** 我都好高興認識你。

---

## Key Patterns

### Asking someone's name

**你叫乜嘢名？** `nei5 giu3 mat1 je5 meng2?`

### Saying your name

**我叫＿＿＿。**

### Asking "what about you?"

**你呢？** `nei5 ne1?`

### Starting a polite question

**請問...** `cing2 man6 ...`

---

## Side Note for Mandarin Speakers

Some common Cantonese words in this lesson have close Mandarin equivalents:

- **乜嘢 (mat1 je5)** ≈ **什么 (shénme)**
- **名 (meng2)** ≈ **名字 (míngzi)**

Many basic words and sentence patterns will feel familiar, although the pronunciation is often very different.
