## Grammar

### The Verb 係 (hai6)

The Cantonese verb 係 means "to be."

Examples:

**你係老師嗎？** `nei5 hai6 lou5 si1 maa3`

[audio:verb-hai-example-1|你係老師嗎？]

(Are you a teacher?)

**我係老師。** `ngo5 hai6 lou5 si1`

[audio:verb-hai-example-2|我係老師。]

(I am a teacher.)

**佢係學生。** `keoi5 hai6 hok6 saang1`

[audio:verb-hai-example-3|佢係學生。]

(He/She is a student.)

係 is usually negated with 唔.

---

### Ending Questions with 嗎 (maa3)

A simple way to form a yes/no question is to add 嗎 at the end.

Examples:

**你係老師嗎？** `nei5 hai6 lou5 si1 maa3`

[audio:question-example-1|你係老師嗎？]

(Are you a teacher?)

**你係學生嗎？** `nei5 hai6 hok6 saang1 maa3`

[audio:question-example-2|你係學生嗎？]

(Are you a student?)

In casual speech, many speakers may use 呀 (aa3) or 啊 (aa) instead.

---

### The Negation Adverb 唔 (m4)

唔 means "not."

Examples:

**我唔係北京人。** `ngo5 m4 hai6 bak1 ging1 jan4`

[audio:negation-example-1|我唔係北京人。]

(I am not from Beijing.)

**佢唔係中國人。** `keoi5 m4 hai6 zung1 gwok3 jan4`

[audio:negation-example-2|佢唔係中國人。]

(He/She is not Chinese.)

---

### The Adverb 都 (dou1)

都 means "also."

Examples:

**我都係學生。** `ngo5 dou1 hai6 hok6 saang1`

[audio:dou-example-1|我都係學生。]

(I am also a student.)

阿明係學生，阿美都係學生。

[audio:dou-example-2|阿明係學生，阿美都係學生。]

(Ah Ming is a student, and Ah Mei is also a student.)

你係中國人，我都係中國人。

[audio:dou-example-3|你係中國人，我都係中國人。]

(You are Chinese, and I am Chinese too.)

When 都 is used together with 唔, 都 comes before 唔:

阿明唔係老師，阿美都唔係老師。

[audio:dou-example-4|阿明唔係老師，阿美都唔係老師。]

(Ah Ming is not a teacher, and Ah Mei is not a teacher either.)

---

## Side Note for Mandarin Speakers

Some common Cantonese words in this lesson differ noticeably from Mandarin:

- 係 (hai6) ≈ 是 (shì)
- 唔係 (m4 hai6) ≈ 不是 (bú shì)
- 唔 (m4) ≈ 不 (bù)

Another important difference is that spoken Cantonese usually uses 都 (dou1) where Mandarin often uses 也 (yě).

- **我都係學生。** ≈ 我也是學生。
- **我都唔係老師。** ≈ 我也不是老師。

---

## Native Speech Note

If you spend time listening to Hong Kong Cantonese, you may notice that some speakers pronounce 你 (nei5) more like lei5.

For example:

你係老師呀？ nei5 hai6 lou5 si1 aa3? 

(Are you a teacher)

[audio:native-note-nei|你係老師呀？]

may sound closer to:

lei5 hai6 lou5 si1 aa3

[audio:test-lei|李係老師呀？]
This pronunciation is very common in casual speech, especially in Hong Kong. Both pronunciations are widely understood.

For consistency, this course uses the standard Jyutping pronunciation nei5 in lessons, quizzes, and audio.
